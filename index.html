Martin Luther King Jr

Freedom Fighter

Here's a time line of Martin Luther King Jr:

  • 1929 - Born on January 15, in Atlanta, Georgia, U.S.
  • 1942 - King was 13 years old, he became the youngest assistant manager of a newspaper delivery station for the Atlanta Journal.
  • 1944 - n his junior year, King gave his first public speech during an oratorical contest, sponsored by the Improved Benevolent and Protective Order of Elks of the World in Dublin, Georgia. In his speech he stated, black America still wears chains. The finest negro is at the mercy of the meanest white man. Even winners of our highest honors face the class color bar. King was selected as the winner of the contest.
  • 1951 - King graduated with a B.Div. degree from Crozer Theological Seminary in Upland, Pennsylvania.
  • 1953 - Married Coretta Scott.
  • 1955 - He became the most visible spokesperson and leader in the civil rights movement. King led the 1955 Montgomery bus boycott and later became the first president of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC).
  • 1958 - surived knife attack from a mentally ill black woman Izola Curry.
  • 1959 - after being based in Montgomery for five years, King announced his return to Atlanta at the request of the SCLC.
  • 1963 - President of the SCLC, he led the unsuccessful Albany Movement in Albany, Georgia, and helped organize some of the nonviolent 1963 protests in Birmingham, Alabama. King helped organize the 1963 March on Washington, where he delivered his famous "I Have a Dream" speech on the steps of the Lincoln Memorial.
  • 1964 - King won the Nobel Peace Prize for combating racial inequality through nonviolent resistance.
  • 1965 - He helped organize two of the three Selma to Montgomery marches. In his final years, he expanded his focus to include opposition towards poverty, capitalism, and the Vietnam War. He was also awarded the American Liberties Medallion by the American Jewish Committee for his "exceptional advancement of the principles of human liberty." In his acceptance remarks, King said,Freedom is one thing. You have it all or you are not free.
  • 1968 - Died in Memphis, Tennessee, U.S.
